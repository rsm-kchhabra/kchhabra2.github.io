Exploring Market Insights Through Penguins, Yogurt, and KNN: A Multimodal Data Science Report

Executive Summary

This report delivers insights across four analytical challenges using supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques. The tasks span consumer choice modeling, species clustering, synthetic classification, and satisfaction drivers. Through datasets on penguin morphology, yogurt preferences, synthetic behaviors, and driver-brand evaluations, we demonstrate how statistical modeling and machine learning uncover meaningful structure in both natural and commercial systems.

1. Unsupervised Learning Analysis 1a: K-Means Clustering on Penguins

We implemented a custom K-Means algorithm from scratch using two variables:

  • bill_length_mm
  • flipper_length_mm

Objective

To group penguins into natural clusters and evaluate clustering performance using:

  • WCSS (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares)
  • Silhouette Scores

Key Insights

  • Both metrics suggest K=3 provides a natural segmentation.
  • Our implementation’s results align closely with scikit-learn’s KMeans.

Conclusion

This comprehensive analysis demonstrates:

  • The power of unsupervised learning to uncover natural structure in both biological and consumer data
  • The flexibility of supervised models like KNN and regression in handling nonlinear boundaries and business-focused prediction
